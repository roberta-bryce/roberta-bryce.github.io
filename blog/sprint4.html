23/09/22 Sprint 4 Problem Solving
A time you were blocked on a simple problem:

The Built-in Methods ‘Where Are You?’ Kata had two different problems that I’m sure were simple but for some reason my brain couldn’t compute. First I was unable to figure out the name of the object, and second I couldn’t get anything working outside of the function.

I actually never figured out the name of the object. I checked console.log(arrayOfObjects) in the whereAreYou_test.js file and tried the names I thought could apply cycling through them. With no luck. I thought I needed the name of the object as you need to call the whereAreYou function outside of itself to use the find method, and once I left the whereAreYou function arrayOfObjects was not defined…

This also leads to my second problem of I couldn’t code outside of my functions, it would cause the test program to have a wobble and interfere with other functions. This meant I couldn’t call the function while using the find method and couldn’t use the object name even if I did work it out. I tried rewriting my code with small alterations but nothing was working.

So rather than solving those two problems separately I combined them as the solution needed was ‘how do I get everything working within the function?’ This led to a lot of googling and a lot of youtube tutorials until I found an example of using the find method in one line using a fat arrow, which I updated with the correct details for the object:

const result = arrayOfObjects.find(({ hiding }) => hiding === 'Scooby');

Worked a treat! Felt very satisfying to get it working. Throughout the process I was annoyed that my issue involved working outside a function as normally that wouldn’t be an issue, also this looked deceptively easy so I felt a bit bad tripping up on this question. That was all backburner though, for the most part my emotions were focused on reaching the finish line of the Katas.

Overall I learnt that perseverance works. I knew the solution was out there, it just took time to get there, and along the way my understanding of the find method improved so it’s no loss.

 An elegantly solved a problem:

The Kata of FizzBuzz was my smoothest coding experience this Sprint.

I kept the problem explanation as pseudocode at the top to refer to. Even with the way the problem was phrased referenced using a conditional. So I used my favourite if/else.

So I coded it up looking good, but still bringing up an error. I rubber duckie-d it and yeah I felt really good about my maths so what was happening? Turned to my bff google and immediately found out the issue I was using divide rather than remainder - forgot I was talking to a computer and not a maths teacher. Once I changed my divides over to remainders it FizzBuzzed like a champion.

Throughout the process I felt pretty keyed in, so I didn’t really keep tabs of emotions.

On this one I learnt to respect my code for the dumb robot that she is and got a bit more comfortable with remainders.

19/09/22 Sprint 4 Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset
Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

By understanding the learning process more it is easier to interpret progress and be more forgiving as we grow. Neuroplasticity teaches us ways to approach learning a new subject; for example the ‘Use it or Lose it’/’Use it and Improve it’ mentioned by cfimove.org. These principles are all focused on repetition - the more we practice a new concept or skill the more our brain will get used to it and we will change from having to fumble our way through to eventually streamlining and innovating the same skill. By understanding the process we can account for it in our learning to get the most out of our time.

 Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit

Pretty similar answer to above, by understanding neuroplasticity I can lean on its principles to get the most out of my learning. By emphasizing repetition, focus, and meaning I can approach my learning with intent. There is also an emphasis with neuroplasticity that learning new things is hard and that it will feel like an uphill battle, but that is okay and part of the process.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Although neuroplasticity is about the learning process, the concept in practice is more about holistic wellbeing. The website Neurohacker discusses a few ways to increase neuroplasticity; first and foremost is to be in a good headspace. Looking after yourself by sleeping right, exercising and relaxing will in turn benefit neuroplasticity as it all creates space for cognitive growth.

 Discuss what a Growth Mindset is and why it is relevant.

Growth mindset is about keeping an open mind about yourself and others. Rather than putting yourself in a box and limiting yourself, the growth mindset is about how everyone can change and develop.

Through acknowledging this change it then becomes easier as you are in a state of openness and encouragement. Growth Mindset is incredibly relevant as we enter Sprint 4 of foundations as we are just beginning our journey from changing from ‘not a web developer’ to a ‘web developer’ and it can be easy to say I can’t do it or I can’t think like that but instead we need to focus on that fact that we are learning and that we eventually can.

 In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

It was mostly affirming. I was struggling towards the end of Sprint 3 as I was definitely feeling the limitations of what I can do. But, through looking into neuroplasticity and growth mindset I felt better as this is part of the process! The more we study, understand and practice the closer we’ll get to feeling competent in our new skills, especially while being mindful of how we are learning.

 How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

Definitely through mindfulness. By approaching this learning journey with intent and openness it will help overcome the downright hard task of learning how to code. Everyone has to go through their own journey to get good at something and we need to keep a growth mindset as we meet obstacles along the way.
