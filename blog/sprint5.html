What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Well first is that core learning, or rather sharing these details, makes me uncomfortable. Secondly is that I need to harness more of that growth mindset, it’s hard to let go of self-imposed limitations. Thirdly; the learning plan was a useful layout for what my intentions are for the course and something that I like to refer back to when I need.

 What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Being able to perceive and acknowledge our own and others emotions is integral to working well with others.

Throughout this course we’ll be working in teams in a stressful environment. Then later when we graduate we will continue to work with others to meet deadlines. Being attuned to values, empathy and self-awareness is advantageous for learning, programming, and life overall.

 What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Nothing really 'surprised' me, but Learning about neuroplasticity was cool.

I like being able to conceptualize the learning process better i.e. walking over an overgrown path till it’s paved. I think this will be useful going forward.

 What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Trying to be open and sharing about these subjects. I’m still very uncomfortable but I understand that this is a part of the curriculum and try my best.

 Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Well no one works in isolation, and I imagine programming is particularly team based.

The better we get at understanding others and ourselves the better we can work in a team. So, hopefully at the end of this course we emerge as promising well-rounded web developers.

 Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

It’s not my cup of tea, but I'm just a student so I’m trusting the process.

The core work is good for variety, as sometimes it’s nice to take a break from tech and still be up to something productive. Overall the devacademy folks seem really proud of this aspect of the course so I’m happy to follow the path.
